In the photograph on my previous post of Beppu city, you may have seen plumes of steam rising up from the ground, this is because Beppu is on top of a very large volcanic area.<\/p>\n

<\/a>Our next stop was at a place where several large steam and water vents come up from this volcanic activity creating spectacular places to view the steam and water coming out.<\/p>\n

<\/a>This particular location has at least eight different events that can be viewed and they are known as the hells of Beppu.<\/p>\n

<\/a>The water is extremely hot and not safe for anyone to try and bathe in. \u00a0However Japanese have surrounded the spots with incredible, beautiful nature which they keep up meticulously .<\/p>\n

<\/a>This tied back into our team time this morning as we were reminded that in Nagasaki during the big persecution of the Christian church, the believers were sometimes thrown into the boiling waters to be killed.<\/p>\n

<\/a>We had read, \u201cHow precious in your eyes is the death of your faithful servants\u201d. \u00a0We saw two things this morning as we read that verse. \u00a0 One, that God \u00a0notices and it was precious to him. \u00a0The \u00a0other was the faithfulness and commitment of the Japanese who choose death, rather than deny their faith in Jesus .<\/p>\n

<\/a>Just before leaving the park we took advantage of the onsen foot \u00a0soaking area where the water wasn\u2019t too hot and we could relax and soothe our feet before the journey home.<\/p>\n
