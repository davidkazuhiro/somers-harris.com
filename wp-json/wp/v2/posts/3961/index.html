Driving away from Beppu station we went directly to our first Onsen – sorry no pictures. \u00a0It was a truly amazing place with the baths up on the roof giving an excellent view of the city. \u00a0We arrived before sunset so were able to take in both the daylight and the night time views.<\/p>\n

In the onsen section there was a cave type room, when Jose, Tim and I went inside we played with singing different notes which reverberated around the cave. \u00a0 Then we decided to sing amazing Grace together. \u00a0As we were singing a father and his young son came in who were from Hong Kong, the father asked if we were singing amazing Grace. \u00a0We said yes so we sang the first verse again and he and his son sang along. \u00a0A Japanese \u00a0young man from Fukuoka also came into the cave and he too sang along with us. \u00a0 Even though he was not a Christian he somehow knew the song. \u00a0When the young boy from Hong Kong left the cave we could hear him singing amazing Grace. \u00a0It was a precious moment.<\/p>\n

There was also a swimming pool that was part of this complex so we donned \u00a0our swimming costumes and went outside. \u00a0Ellie and Julianne joined us there and we swam for a bit then entererd anothe cave like structue that had salt water in it. \u00a0Jose and Tim enjoyed floating from one side to the other.<\/p>\n

Going back outside we \u00a0relaxed while enjoying a wonderful light and water display the pool people put on in the evenings. \u00a0It was a most pleasant way to finish our day abd we felt very refreshed.<\/p>\n
